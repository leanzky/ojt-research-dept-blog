19/06/2023

Mon ●Week 1

  • collapse expand

It's June 19th, Monday. I enlisted myself as an intern/OJT under Prof. Jane B. Burce. We were interviewed and briefed on what is about to happen during the entire OJT time frame and what to expect. During the interview, ma'am asked us to introduce ourselves and share our skills in software development. She also had us choose our roles in the software development process, as she wants us to develop software throughout the OJT session. After meeting my fellow interns in the room, Professor Jane asked us to accommodate our block mates. It turns out she's also the block OJT coordinator. She briefed the class about the available slots in other departments where interns should also apply. The rest of what she said was the same as what she told us firsthand.

20/06/2023

Tue ●Week 1

  • collapse expand

Tuesday, June 20th. Professor Jane requested our assistance in preparing accreditation documents being handled by interns under Sir Ryan A. Rodriguez. And so, we began working on the documents for our department's accreditation. It seems that Sir Ryan is short on interns. My block mates didn't find the position under Sir Ryan appealing. Throughout the day, we prepared the necessary documents for accreditation. Some of us printed the documents, while others cut out papers for tabbing. A few of us even learned how to soft bind papers using the machine provided by Sir Ryan. As the day progressed, we kept working, and when the clock struck 5, we signed the logbook and headed home.

21/06/2023

Wed ●Week 1

  • collapse expand

Wednesday, June 21st: It's Bicol University Charter Day! We participated in a foot parade from BU to Albay Astrodome. When we arrived, a celebration awaited us. Different departments of BU occupied the colored chairs in the wide-open space under the gymnasium. On the other hand, we interns occupied the ones on the side. A mass formally commenced the event, followed by messages from notable and important people from BU.

22/06/2023

Thu ●Week 1

  • collapse expand

Thursday, June 22nd. Ma’am Jane met us for some talks. She wanted us to decide who among our group should be assigned to Sir Ryan, as sir Ryan is currently short for interns. The meeting went on and I was among the options who should be moved. The final decision was concluded after drawing the names of the interns in a magic wheel app. My name was among the chosen along with Kyle Roque. At the end, we will now be working to sir Ryan. The rest of the day went on, we prepared the necessary documents that should be prepared. I did what the others were not able to as they were busy with other tasks. I was just cutting out papers for the tabbing of documents. Afterwards, I helped my co-interns in the document tabbing.

23/06/2023

Fri ●Week 1

  • collapse expand

Friday, it’s the 23rd day of June. The head of the technical maintenance department went to our office and told us he needed two interns to assist him in networking. I gladly volunteered as it was more exciting than preparing documents. And probably, the skills I could get there could help me more than printing documents in the printer. I gained more experience in preparing RJ45 ethernet connections for distributing internet connection in the laboratory. The day seemed to be more productive than when I am at the office.

26/06/2023

Mon●Week 2

  • collapse expand

It’s the 2nd week of our OJT. Following my last entry in this daily blog, I was assigned at Sir Ryan’s office. Primarily, we should be preparing documents needed for accreditation necessities but since this task wouldn’t need that much number of OJTs I’m now assisting the head maintenance of our department. Today, we continued with making new RJ45 connections for the computer in the laboratory. Some of us are preparing the RJ45 wires, some are near the utility rack where a switch is attached and requiring human intervention for patching of wires. There are 25 computers in the laboratory each of which we have to make connections for. Today, we finished the first 2 rows of computers which is about 16 computers.

27/06/2023

Tue●Week 2

  • collapse expand

We continued with the work we left yesterday. We made RJ45 connections for the remaining computers where connections are not yet established. Since there are only one row of computers left, it didn’t take too long before we finished. The next task that we were working on is the patching of wires on the networking device called switch. The work is not simple as it seems. But despite the complicated setup we were kind of enjoying what we’re doing. The connections are now finished from individual computers to the utility rack where the internet connection distribution is done by the device switch.

29/06/2023

Thu ●Week 2

  • collapse expand

Its Thursday today of our 2nd week in OJT. We finished the task of establishing new RJ45 connections for the computer laboratory of our department, the rest of the task would now be allocated for the wire management of the 3 rows of computer. The wires currently are entangled to each other, untangling them was not a simple task since we have to unplug all the connections and arrange them in an order where they would not seem like a knot. We finished this task today but, we run into some problems. Some of the wire connections are apparently shorter than the others. We were able to fix some of the tangled wires, but since there are wires that were not long enough for moving of the utility rack where it was connected to, we have to start over from the top. Renew some of the connections and arrange these new connections accordingly.

30/06/2023

Fri ●Week 2

  • collapse expand

Friday, this marks the end of our 2nd week in the OJT program of our course. Today, since we finished the task yesterday, we were given a new one. We were instructed to clone all of the hard disk drive there is in the computer laboratory from a given source. The objective is to set a computer content similar to all the other computer. Instead of formatting the machines, they were just cloned so we will be spared of the task of installing the needed software one by one.
